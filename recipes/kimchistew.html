Kimchi Soup (Kimchi Jjigae)

Kimchi Soup (Kimchi Jjigae) is a classic Korean comfort food made with tangy, fermented kimchi, tofu, and savory broth. It's quick, flavorful, and perfect for cold weather or when you're craving something warm and satisfying. A must-try for kimchi lovers!


Ingredients

  • 2 cups well-fermented kimchi (chopped into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1/2 lb pork belly or beef (optional, cut into small pieces)
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean red chili paste) or gochugaru (chili flakes)
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 block tofu (firm or silken, cut into cubes)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance the acidity)
  • 2 green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ingredients. Chop the kimchi into bite-sized pieces. If using pork belly or beef, cut it into small chunks.
  2. Slice the onion, mince the garlic, and cut the tofu into cubes.
  3. Cook the Meat (if using). In a large pot, heat sesame oil over medium heat. Add the pork belly or beef and cook until browned and crispy, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Make the Soup Base. Add the sliced onion and minced garlic to the pot, and saut√© for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  5. Stir in the gochujang or gochugaru and cook for another minute to release the flavors.
  6. Add the Kimchi and Broth. Add the chopped kimchi to the pot and stir. Pour in the broth and soy sauce. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat to a simmer.
  7. Let the soup simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to blend together.
  8. Add Tofu and Season. Gently add the tofu cubes to the pot and simmer for an additional 5 minutes.
  9. Taste the soup and add sugar, salt, or extra soy sauce if needed to balance the flavors.
  10. Serve. Ladle the hot kimchi soup into bowls. Garnish with chopped green onions and serve with steamed rice on the side.
  11. Enjoy the spicy, savory goodness of this delicious Korean soup!
